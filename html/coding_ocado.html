Ocado Technology (2010 - 2015)

Ocado is an online supermarket based just outside London. The company has always written most of its IT systems in-house, and the technology department has grown to become almost a separate company.

I worked as a Software Engineer on a variety of projects, mainly web-based applications for in-house staff. I designed and built several systems from concept to post-deployment support, both alone and in teams ranging from two to ten people.

I worked in Java and SQL, using Tomcat and GlassFish servers on Linux VMs as well as Cloud-based servers. I gained experience of database design, iterative user interface development, XML and JSON messaging, HTTP services, dependency injection frameworks, Scrum Agile development practice and Test Driven Development. I became a Clean Coder of the 'Uncle Bob' variety.

I worked on several large Java applications with GXT front-ends, a cross-area testing team prior to the opening of Ocado's second Customer Fulfillment Centre, and a major replatforming project using cloud-based micro-services prior to the company's first international business-to-business venture. I provided technical support to users at Ocado and Morrisons, and liaised with other external companies. I received training in computer security and anti-competition legislation.

I also contributed to some in-house frameworks we developed for our applications, and which were subsequently used by other teams. One of them is now open source, and I still use it my own projects.

Ocado Council Representative for Technology

From 2013 I was a representative for the Technology department on the business's employee council, representing the concerns of my constituents to the highest levels of management. I received ACAS training for disciplinary and grievance resolution, and learned techniques for effective meeting facilitation, discussion and decision making. I also gained quite a reputation for the regular entertaining update communications I sent out to my constituents, and I like to think I had some influence on the dramatic increase in constituent participation in Council affairs that occurred during my tenure.
