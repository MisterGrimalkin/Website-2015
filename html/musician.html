Past

I've been around music my entire life. My first stage performance was on drums, aged eight, but I was quickly seduced by the piano and the synthesizer. I was the 'odd kid' in the heavily classically-oriented music department at school, because I played in bands and wrote my own music. My classical training ended after my A-levels. I didn't study music at university, but I carried on playing keyboards in a pop/rock band and couple of metal bands while I was a student. I even, once, played a gig wearing corpse-paint. Only once. Only ever once.

After university I ran a music rehearsal and recording studio in Birmingham with my brother Martyn. I was the resident engineer/producer and occasional session keyboardist for various local acts who recorded with us over the years. Hockley Street Studios is still going, though I parted ways with the business when I moved to London in 2010.

I met many fantastic musicians over the eight years I was at Hockley Street, and had the pleasure of jamming and performing with a lot of them. With so many influences my playing developed and I began to find my own style. I learned a lot about music theory, and even got into jazz (which is a lot more fun to play than to listen to). I played and recorded extensively with electronica/ska band Circadia and jazz/funk/psychedelic band Snooty Bobs.

Since moving to London I've played with jazz/soul band Soulmaze, cabaret jazz band Winnet, a couple of function bands (no!) and, of course, the ska/punk covers super-group-extraordinaire Less Than A Real Big Gold Fish Finger.

I recently produced an EP for ska band Call Me Malcolm, and produced a few soundtrack projects for Two Twenty Two Music.
